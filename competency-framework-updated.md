# 1. Product Thinking & Prioritization

Strategic product thinking, problem framing, and prioritization capabilities

## 1.1 Feature Definition & Prioritization

### P1 Entry
- Asks clarifying questions to understand user and business needs
- Contributes observations during discovery sessions
- Participates in opportunity mapping using provided frameworks
- Breaks work into simple tasks with support from PMs and senior designers

### P2 Developing
- Writes concise problem statements and hypotheses for assigned features
- Recognizes patterns in recurring user needs across related features
- Aligns feature scope with PMs and engineers based on constraints
- Explains rationale for prioritization suggestions to the triad

### P3 Career
- Facilitates structured problem framing sessions (e.g., OST, opportunity mapping)
- Synthesizes research and data into well-defined opportunity areas
- Makes tradeoffs visible and well-reasoned across user, business, and technical needs
- Shapes feature scope and priorities through cross-functional alignment

### P4 Advanced
- Facilitates multi-team opportunity mapping and problem definition
- Identifies gaps in problem framing across product areas and resolves them
- Mentors others on prioritization frameworks and tradeoff analysis
- Aligns UX investments with roadmap strategy across teams

### P5 Principal
- Creates and adapts prioritization frameworks used organization-wide
- Aligns multi-year UX vision with strategic opportunities
- Shapes portfolio-level investment decisions based on user and business value
- Ensures teams across the organization have clarity on problems worth solving

## 1.2 Analytical Thinking & Problem Solving

### P1 Entry
- Breaks down simple problems into steps using provided frameworks
- Asks clarifying questions to understand goals and constraints
- Applies basic logical reasoning to evaluate design options
- Documents basic rationale for design decisions when asked

### P2 Developing
- Decomposes ambiguous problems into manageable parts independently
- Uses research and data to support prioritization decisions
- Applies structured methods (e.g., 5 Whys, assumption mapping) to test thinking
- Collaborates with peers to refine problem definitions and share reasoning

### P3 Career
- Deconstructs complex, multi-faceted problems into actionable work streams
- Synthesizes insights from multiple sources into clear decision inputs
- Establishes measurable success criteria and documents tradeoffs and risks
- Anticipates downstream implications and communicates them to stakeholders

### P4 Advanced
- Guides teams through complex problem spaces with structured approaches
- Introduces new analytical tools and frameworks to elevate team reasoning
- Identifies systemic root causes that span multiple product areas
- Coaches teams on data-driven iteration and structured decision-making

### P5 Principal
- Defines scalable models for problem decomposition used across the organization
- Aligns strategy with structured understanding of user value at portfolio level
- Guides complex tradeoff decisions that affect multi-year product direction
- Mentors organizational leadership on decision-making techniques and frameworks

## 1.3 AI-Augmented Product Thinking

### P1 Entry
- Uses AI tools to summarize research findings and competitor information
- Explores AI-generated ideas as a starting point for design concepts
- Follows established prompt patterns to extract basic product insights from AI tools
- Understands when AI suggestions are relevant vs. off-target for a given problem

### P2 Developing

- Uses AI to generate and stress-test multiple product hypotheses
- Crafts effective prompts to extract meaningful product insights from AI tools

### P3 Career
- Integrates AI into the product thinking workflow to rapidly explore problem spaces
- Uses AI to identify edge cases, accessibility considerations, and unmet user needs
- Critically evaluates AI outputs and iterates on prompts to improve relevance
- Combines AI insights with qualitative research to strengthen prioritization decisions

### P4 Advanced
- Develops AI-augmented product strategy frameworks adopted by the team
- Identifies opportunities where AI can transform product discovery processes
- Mentors others on leveraging AI for product thinking and prioritization
- Balances AI efficiency with human judgment in strategic product decisions

### P5 Principal
- Shapes the organization's approach to AI-augmented product strategy
- Pioneers novel AI-assisted methods for identifying market opportunities
- Publishes or presents on AI-enhanced product thinking methodologies
- Drives cross-functional adoption of AI tools for product decision-making

# 2. Visual, Interaction & Content Design

Encompasses the skills needed to create intuitive, accessible, and visually cohesive user experiences across interaction design, information architecture, and inclusive design practices.

## 2.1 Interaction & Visual Design Execution

### P1 Entry
- Creates mockups, simple flows, and prototypes using existing components and styles
- Applies spacing, hierarchy, and layout rules with coaching
- Asks for feedback early to ensure alignment with established patterns
- Documents basic screens or states when asked for handoff

### P2 Developing
- Produces clear end-to-end flows that consider standard states (empty, loading, errors)
- Applies design principles for hierarchy, alignment, contrast, and interaction patterns
- Contributes improvements to reusable components and micro-interactions
- Communicates intent clearly through annotations, specs, and prototype behaviors

### P3 Career
- Designs multi-step, multi-state flows that anticipate edge cases and failure modes
- Makes thoughtful fidelity tradeoffs appropriate to project stage (low to high)
- Ensures visual and interaction consistency across a domain or feature set
- Collaborates closely with engineering to refine implementation details

### P4 Advanced
- Reviews others' work to maintain coherence, quality, and adherence to standards
- Identifies design gaps and evolves patterns or principles to address them
- Ensures accessibility, responsiveness, and performance considerations are integrated
- Leads design QA processes to ensure smooth implementation and polish

### P5 Principal
- Establishes long-term experience patterns and visual direction for the organization
- Identifies areas where design quality or clarity can be elevated at scale
- Creates principles that guide cross-product cohesion, motion, and interaction
- Influences cross-functional teams on design vision with clarity and authority

## 2.2 Information Architecture (IA) & Content Design / UX Writing

### P1 Entry
- Organizes simple content structures (labels, lists, menus) using existing patterns
- Writes or adapts microcopy using established tone and examples
- Follows templates for naming, hierarchy, and navigation
- Ensures content is understandable and concise with reviewer support

### P2 Developing
- Maps basic content structures and screen flows, ensuring logical hierarchy
- Writes microcopy (buttons, labels, empty states) with clarity and consistency
- Identifies content redundancies or ambiguities in flows
- Collaborates with PM/engineering to refine naming, labeling, and terminology

### P3 Career
- Defines navigation models, content hierarchies, and flow structure for complex features
- Writes effective UX copy that guides, educates, or reassures users
- Balances brevity, clarity, voice, and task success when crafting content
- Ensures content aligns with user mental models and reduces cognitive load

### P4 Advanced
- Establishes guidelines for naming, navigation, terminology, and content consistency
- Identifies structural issues in content architecture across teams and resolves them
- Coaches designers in crafting high-quality UX copy and structuring flows
- Partners with PMs, CS, and Marketing to ensure cohesive terminology across the product

### P5 Principal
- Creates long-term content systems and IA frameworks used across product lines
- Ensures consistent UX writing principles and semantic structures across teams
- Advocates for content strategy as a critical pillar of user experience
- Sets standards for information clarity, naming systems, and conceptual models org-wide

## 2.3 Accessibility & Inclusive Design

### P1 Entry
- Uses contrast checkers, alt-text guidelines, and basic WCAG references
- Follows established accessibility patterns in components and flows
- Identifies obvious accessibility risks (tiny text, low contrast, unclear labels)
- Seeks support when unsure how to make a design accessible

### P2 Developing
- Designs UI elements and flows that follow WCAG basics (contrast, focus, semantics)
- Uses tools (color-blindness simulators, keyboard navigation checks) to validate accessibility
- Writes clear labels, instructions, and microcopy to support assistive technologies
- Flags accessibility gaps during critique or implementation reviews

### P3 Career
- Applies WCAG 2.1 AA systematically when designing complex interactions
- Anticipates cognitive, visual, and motor accessibility needs across flows
- Partners with engineering to ensure accessible behavior during implementation
- Facilitates inclusive usability testing and integrates insights into improvements

### P4 Advanced
- Reviews team designs for accessibility quality and consistency
- Identifies system-level accessibility issues and leads remediation efforts
- Ensures design system components meet accessibility standards and are tested
- Mentors designers and engineers on inclusive design principles and techniques

### P5 Principal
- Defines org-wide accessibility standards, policies, and quality targets
- Ensures inclusive design is integrated into product strategy and planning
- Represents accessibility in cross-functional leadership forums and decision-making
- Drives multi-year initiatives that elevate inclusive design maturity at scale

## 2.4 AI-Augmented Design Execution

### P1 Entry
- Uses AI tools to generate initial visual explorations and design variations
- Applies AI to draft and iterate on UI copy and microcopy
- Follows established prompt patterns to generate basic design alternatives
- Recognizes the difference between AI-generated output and production-ready design

### P2 Developing
- Leverages AI to rapidly explore layout options, color palettes, and typography
- Iterates on AI-generated content to match brand voice and UX writing standards
- Uses AI to generate interaction patterns and evaluate alternatives
- Adapts AI outputs to align with existing design system components and patterns

### P3 Career
- Integrates AI into the design workflow for rapid prototyping and iteration cycles
- Uses AI to generate accessible design alternatives and identify inclusivity gaps
- Develops effective prompt strategies for consistent, high-quality design outputs
- Critically curates AI-generated work, applying design judgment to refine results

### P4 Advanced
- Creates AI-enhanced design workflows that elevate team productivity and quality
- Develops reusable prompt libraries and AI templates for common design patterns
- Mentors designers on effective use of AI for visual, interaction, and content design
- Pushes the boundaries of AI-assisted design while maintaining craft standards

### P5 Principal
- Defines the organization's vision for AI-augmented design execution
- Pioneers new AI-design methodologies that influence industry practice
- Establishes quality standards for AI-assisted design outputs across the organization
- Drives strategic partnerships with AI tool providers to advance design capabilities

# 5. Technical Fluency

- Communicates clearly with engineers during handoff
- Identifies simple feasibility concerns and asks for guidance

### P2 Developing

- Designs with feasibility and performance constraints in mind
- Participates in code walkthroughs or Storybook reviews
- Raises engineering risks during planning and adjusts designs accordingly

### P3 Career
- Anticipates implementation challenges and adjusts design patterns proactively
- Balances fidelity, complexity, and performance tradeoffs in solutions
- Helps define component behavior and internal logic with engineering
- Spots opportunities for engineering simplification without compromising UX

### P4 Advanced
- Creates guidance and tools to support scalable implementation across teams
- Mentors designers on engineering-aware decision-making
- Partners with engineering leads to improve token and component architecture
- Ensures consistency between design specifications and front-end systems

### P5 Principal

- Drives investments in tokens, components, and design infrastructure

### P2 Developing

### P3 Career
- Uses AI coding tools to implement UI and usability improvements across features independently
- Submits well-scoped PRs for design fidelity fixes, component behavior adjustments, and small features
- Identifies patterns in recurring UI issues and fixes them systematically in the codebase
- Partners with engineering to ensure design-driven code changes meet quality and performance standards

### P4 Advanced
- Champions designer-submitted PRs as a practice and coaches others in using AI coding tools effectively
- Establishes patterns and guidelines for designers contributing code changes across teams
- Uses AI coding tools to prototype and ship cross-cutting UI and usability improvements
- Removes barriers between design and engineering by normalizing direct code contributions from designers

### P5 Principal
- Shapes the organization's approach to designers as code contributors using AI tools
- Defines governance and quality standards for designer-submitted PRs at scale
- Drives investment in AI coding tool workflows that accelerate design-to-code iteration
- Aligns design and engineering practices so that designers can directly improve product quality across the organization
